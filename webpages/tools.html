Tools

Hasnain created an organization and a repository for Team Phoenix members to join with admin access, that allowed for the cloning, pulling and pushing of repositories. As William primarily handled the development of the group website, all progressive updates to the site were tested on a live local environment before commits were pushed to the main directory. The Audit Log reflects this, accurately depicting what few merge requests there were. The commit trail reveals steady progression over the past week before the deadline with regular activity.

Timestamps correctly show an uneven and unplanned distribution of duties, there unfortunately was not a collaborative effort on the website and there is only activity on four separate days. These records only account for the creation of the organization and repository, invites to members and revision of member privileges. What this log doesn’t consider is the individual amount of time and effort put into certain areas unrelated to the website itself, such as the information that was collected and collaborated on. This was due to the website being more of an afterthought with the main focus on the content.
