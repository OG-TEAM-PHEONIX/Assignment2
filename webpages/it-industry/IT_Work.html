IT Work

To gain an understanding of what occurs on a day-to-day basis for an IT professional an interview was conducted with Daniel Stone.

Daniel has been in the IT industry for 7 years. After graduating the University of South Australia with a bachelor's degree (Honors) in Computer Science Daniel began working at Saab Australia as a Software Engineer and is now the Associate Software Functional Manager of Engineering and Technology at Lockheed Martin Australia Rotary and Mission Systems.

Daniel holds a senior position at Lockheed Martin. A typical day consists of both technical and general people leading work with 30 direct reports. The technical tasks include; writing code, moving tickets, attending scrums, acting as either a project lead or working with the project lead, reading specifications, troubleshooting and debugging in the integration lab, helping junior engineers and integrating technology and testing. While the technical duties account for 80% of the roles workload the general tasks account for 50% of Daniels time. These additional management tasks include; timesheet management, interactions with HR, Career development plans, Hiring, Firing, the graduate programme and community programs such as university work, engineers in the classroom and various school-based STEM programmes.

A vital component of Daniels job is internal and external stakeholder management. Internal stakeholders include;

  • Executives
  • Middle management
  • Project leads
  • Software tasking leads
  • Engineers
  • Project managers
  • Internal investors
  • Hardware engineers
  • Systems engineers

Customers and external stakeholders include;

  • Defense force personnel who will be using the technology
  • Senior members of the Australian Defence Force including the Chief of Air Force and the Chief of Navy
  • Government officials
  • Investors
  • Universities
  • Schools

As a Software Manager project delivery is also a significant part of Daniels workload and can often be the most challenging. Problems and errors frequently arise during test deliveries with customers as these projects have previously only been in the research and development prototype stage. Overcoming these challenges with customers and other stakeholders is vital.
